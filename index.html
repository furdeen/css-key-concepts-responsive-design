Fun Fact: In ancient Egyptian households, families often kept domestic cats not only as cherished pets but also as protectors against evil spirits. Cats were believed to have protective qualities, and their presence in homes was thought to bring good luck. Killing a cat, even accidentally, was considered a serious crime and could lead to severe punishment, emphasizing the high regard in which these animals were held. The goddess Bastet, often depicted with the head of a lioness or domestic cat, was the goddess of home, fertility, and protection, further solidifying the special place of cats in ancient Egyptian culture.
